Doctor Class 5e


The Doctor is a unofficial, non-magical utility and healing class for 5e Dungeons and Dragons.When looking at the different character options given by the game there was nothing which filled the desire for a more realistic alternative to healing. After looking for unofficial alternatives and being unhappy with community designs, I endeavored to create a testament to inspiration such as the character "Croaker" from Glen Cook's The Black Company and Gothic works like Mary Shelly's Frankenstein.

The Doctor is designed in a similar structure to the Monk Class. The class works off two resource systems "first aid" and "treatment points". First Aid, is the main resource expended for healing. The ability works as a dice pool that scales with level similar to the Rogue Class's Sneak Attack but can only be used a number of times. The number of the times the Doctor can heal is more limited than other Classes but the amount is always a static, large amount in comparison. The Doctor uses First Aid to heal in large bursts and can expend Treatment Points to heal in smaller amounts. Treatments Points are a catch all resource for most over active abilities in the class and are meant to allow the Doctor to fulfill many of the same roles as other support classes without the use of spells and the need for players to track a large number of cool downs. 

The Doctor Class provides something unique to 5e Dungeons and Dragons and has become one of my favorite projects. The class strengths come from a solid core identity and unique, evoking abilities which capture certain player fantasies. The Apothecary Subclass crafts a large number of potions and allows the player to pursue the most treasured substance, the elixir of life. The Surgeon is able to obtain a large amount of information by cutting apart enemies on and off the battlefield and can at later levels surgically implant monster parts onto their allies. The Combat Medic protects their allies on the front-line, giving orders and providing an supercharge similar games like Team Fortress 2 and Overwatch.

During early playtesting, the Doctor has held up suprisingly well. There have been a little problems when it comes to early game balance. The resource management at earlier levels is taxing and forces the player to make careful choices when spending their treatment points. This problem is parrelleled in other classes who have resource points at early levels. The Doctor's First Aid recharging on a short rest creates a scenario where a players may constantly take short rests to always be healed. I don't believe this is a problem with the class but a problem with the game master. If the player's are constantly taking rests in a dangerous area, it's the game master's job to enforce the reality of the danger. Additionally, although the Doctor could heal all party members, everyone is still being pressed for resources which allow the party to stay competitive in an adventuring day.

The doctor is currently being playtested and edited into a presentable format for publication. The class is on track to be released before the end of 2020.
